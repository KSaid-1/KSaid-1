Personal Home page

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow within the Research School of Astronomy and Astrophysics at the Australian National University. In 2017 I was named the 2017 winner of the IAU and Gruber Foundation Fellowship after receiving my PhD from the University of Cape Town with THREE A's on my PhD examiners reports. This additional grant of 50,000 USD is awarded annually to an extremely promising, young astrophysicist to promote the science of cosmology.

My main research interests are in observational cosmology, Large Scale Structure, and galaxy formation and evolution. I am currently working mainly on The Taipan galaxy survey. Taipan is a multi-object spectroscopic galaxy survey starting observations in 2019 that will cover the whole southern sky and will measure redshifts for 1 million galaxies and peculiar velocities for 100k galaxies in the local universe. Expected results from Taipan include the most accurate measurements of the present-day expansion rate of the universe (the Hubble constant), the creation of the largest maps of density and velocity field of the nearby universe, and enable the testing of Einsteins general theory of relativity.
